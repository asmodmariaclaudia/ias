IOT THREATS

The Internet of Things (IoT) refers to the network of interconnected physical devices embedded with sensors, software, and other technologies that enable them to collect and exchange data over the internet. While IoT brings convenience, automation, and innovation across industries, it also introduces new cybersecurity threats. These threats arise due to the vast number of devices, often with minimal security configurations, making them attractive targets for attackers.

Overview

IoT threats refer to the various cybersecurity risks associated with the use and integration of connected devices in networks. These threats include unauthorized access, data breaches, device hijacking, denial-of-service (DoS) attacks, and the exploitation of software vulnerabilities. The diversity of devices, lack of standardized security protocols, and often-infrequent firmware updates make it difficult to manage and secure the growing IoT ecosystem effectively.
